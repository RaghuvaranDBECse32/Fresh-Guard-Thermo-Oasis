Project Overview

To optimize post-harvest tomato storage, we aim to integrate advanced technology, including Peltier devices, temperature sensors, humidity sensors, and ethylene observers, while prioritizing cost-effectiveness, feasibility, scalability, environmental sustainability, and lifetime storage capabilities.

By leveraging these technologies, we aspire to maintain precise temperature and humidity levels throughout the storage process. Our goal is to achieve this in a manner that is economically viable, adaptable to various scales of operation, environmentally friendly, and capable of ensuring prolonged storage durations without compromising the freshness or quality of the tomatoes.

This project is part of my graduation at Anna University Regional Campus, Tirunelveli, during my final year of B.E. Computer Science and Engineering and our team worked under the mentorship of Dr. J. Roselin. Our team was selected for Level-1 of the Ideation Process in Niral Thiruvizha, conducted by Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation and Naan Mudhalvan, standing out among 144 teams.
